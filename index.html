In My Own Words

A little context behind the metrics and the code.

I didn't become a data analyst by accident — I became one because I genuinely love the moment when a chaotic dataset suddenly tells a story. Whether that's uncovering why a region's revenue is underperforming, catching a data quality issue before it corrupts a regulatory report, or designing a dashboard that a non-technical executive actually reaches for — that's the work I'm here for.

Over 3+ years, I've built analytics workflows across financial services, global technology, and compliance — environments where the stakes of bad data are high. I've owned projects end-to-end: from gathering requirements from Product and Finance teams, to writing the SQL, to deploying the dashboard, to presenting findings to leadership.

I carry a product mindset into everything — I don't just answer the question asked, I challenge whether it's the right question. And I've leaned into AI-powered analytics early: Streamlit apps, AI-assisted modeling, and tools like Snowflake Cortex and Claude Code that help me move faster without sacrificing rigor.
